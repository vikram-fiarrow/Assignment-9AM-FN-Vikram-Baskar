North Korea

The Democratic People's Republic of Korea (DPRK), or North Korea, is a highly isolated, one-party socialist republic under a totalitarian hereditary dictatorship led by Supreme Leader Kim Jong Un. The government exercises strict control over nearly all aspects of public and private life, systematically denying basic liberties, including freedom of expression and movement

North Korea's GDP

North Korea's economy is a centrally planned system with limited market mechanisms, which makes reliable GDP calculation difficult; estimates are often provided by external sources like South Korea's central bank. According to 2024 estimates by the Bank of Korea, North Korea's nominal GDP stands at approximately $32 billion, with a real GDP growth rate of around 3.7%.

North Korea's Festivals

North Korean festivals are primarily state-orchestrated events designed to reinforce the ruling Kim dynasty's personality cult and national ideology. The most significant holidays are the Day of the Sun (April 15th, Kim Il Sung's birthday) and the Day of the Shining Star (February 16th, Kim Jong Il's birthday), which feature grand festivities such as mass parades, fireworks, mass gymnastics (like the Arirang Mass Games, when they are held), and artistic performances.
